New Moon

The New Moon is when the Sun and Moon are aligned, with the Sun and Earth on opposite sides of the Moon. The alignment of the Sun, the Moon, and Earth leaves the side of the Moon that faces Earth in darkness.

Waxing cresent

This intermediate Moon phase comes after New Moon and lasts until half of the Moon's visible surface is illuminated at First Quarter Moon. During this phase, the lit-up part of the Moon increases from 0.1% to 49.9%.

Quater Moon

The First Quarter Moon is a primary Moon phase when half of the Moon's face is lit up. The First Quarter Moon is also called a Half Moon because the Sun's rays illuminate exactly 50% of the Moon's surface.

Waxing Gibbous

This intermediate Moon phase starts after the First Quarter Moon and lasts until the Full Moon. The Waxing Gibbous phase is when the lit-up part of the Moon grows from 50.1% to 99.9%.

Full Moon

Full Moon is the most striking Moon phase when the entire face of the Moon is lit up. The Full Moon is when the Sun and the Moon are aligned on opposite sides of Earth, and 100% of the Moon's face is illuminated by the Sun.
